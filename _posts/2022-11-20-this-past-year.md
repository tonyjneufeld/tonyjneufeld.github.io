This past year has been a great one for my family and me. I have had the opportunity to travel all over Canada and the world. I was able to shift careers into my dream industry, tech. I have reached my goals in sports. I live in a first-world country without war or famine. And even with inflation, I can pay the bills comfortably. It may seem from the outside that things are going perfectly. I have a problem, though.

Let's go back in time a little bit. I am trying to remember what sprung change for me. Still, in March 2021, I started waking up early, journaling religiously, meditating daily, minimizing screen time, reading new books, focusing on purpose and consistently improving myself. 

For that first summer, I found myself in a state of comfort and happiness. I felt productive at work. I found myself seeking out opportunities to help my neighbourhood and community. My mind was clear and sharp. Time moved slowly. 

But as quickly as I started, I fizzled out, and by November 2021, I was back to my "old ways."

For me, my "old ways" were:
1. Wake up late.
2. Have an unproductive morning.
3. Get to work right on time.
4. 3-4 hours of phone screen time, mindlessly scrolling HackerNews, Reddit, or watching the Stock Market
5. Play video games, head to sports, or watch what was trending on Netflix then.
6. Watch YouTube videos.
7. Go to bed.

The "old ways" result in a feeling of comfort in the moment, but after reflecting on things, a sense of lost time. The "old ways" are a state of consumption and a lack of production, even with the fantastic things that have happened in the past year for my family and me. Life and time have quickly passed. I don't want time to "pass me by." Nobody wants that.

I want what I had — productivity, fulfillment, and purpose instead of consumption and comfort. But what I had takes commitment, accountability, focus, and attention. I have no idea how I flipped the switch at that time, but I can, and I will flip the switch again.
